The Geography of Rights:
A 50-State Human Rights Index

A composite index scoring all 50 U.S. states on their protection of human rights across reproductive rights, LGBTQ+ protections, voting access, labor rights, and criminal justice — paired with regression analysis of what actually drives those scores.

Role

Project Manager · Tech Lead

Team

3 (PM + 2 researchers)

Org

UChicago Data Science Society

Term

Spring 2026 · 9 weeks

Overview

What does it actually mean to live in a high-rights state?

Most rights trackers cover one issue at a time. This project aggregates pre-scored data from Guttmacher, MAP, Brennan Center, Oxfam, the Sentencing Project, and others into a single composite Human Rights Policy Index — then tests, with regression, whether political lean, income, urbanization, and demographics actually explain the variation.

Five dimensions, scored 0–100

Reproductive Rights

Abortion legality, shield laws, contraceptive coverage, parental involvement.

LGBTQ+ Protections

MAP policy tally, nondiscrimination, conversion therapy bans, gender-affirming care.

Voting Access

Brennan Center restrictive vs. expansive law counts, voter ID, mail-in access.

Labor Rights

Oxfam BSWI score, minimum wage, paid leave, right-to-work status.

Criminal Justice

Death penalty, cash bail reform, private prison bans, felony disenfranchisement.

Methodological choices that matter

  • Min-max normalization (0–100) for transparency over z-scores.
  • Equal weighting baseline and PCA-derived weights — the comparison is itself a finding.
  • Composite index used as the dependent variable in OLS regression on political and demographic predictors.
  • Spearman rank correlations for robustness against outlier states.
